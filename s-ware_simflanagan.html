Spyware & Keylogging Explained

Keylogging is a type of spyware where a malicious program or application is installed on a device, and every keystroke entered into the computer is secretly recorded and sent to an attacker.

This can be extremely dangerous because attackers may quickly gather passwords, banking information, personal messages, and other sensitive information.

Stolen information may be sold, ransomed, or shared with other malicious organizations and can result in unauthorized access to accounts such as email, banking, and social media.

Spyware is commonly downloaded through phishing emails, Trojan horses, fake downloads, malicious advertisements, or suspicious links.

If you suspect your computer has been infected, contact a reputable IT support company or cybersecurity professional to help remove the malware from your device.

Always be cautious when downloading files or clicking links from unknown sources.
