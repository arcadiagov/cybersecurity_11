You Have Just Fallen Victim to Phishing

You have just experienced what is known as phishing.

By pretending to be a reputable business organization and creating a sense of urgency with a time limit, attackers can convince victims to click dangerous links or download harmful files. Phishing scams use social engineering to convince the user to click on a link or download an attatchment. Social engineering is manipulating people into giving out personal or sensitive information to an attacker, often through either building trust, claiming to be a reputable organisation, including urgency (as was done in the example on the previous page), curiosity, or trust (ex. claiming to be your boss on vacation). Always be very careful whenever interacting with an unknown email adress and verify any links before clicking anything.

In a real cyberattack, clicking a phishing link could have resulted in the installation of:

Virus
Ransomware
Spyware
Worm

Cybersecurity Tip:

Always verify email addresses before clicking on links or downloading attachments. In this scenario, the sender claimed to be CIBC, but the email address was suspicious.

When in doubt, contact the company directly using a trusted phone number or official website instead of clicking links in the email.

Signs That Were Missed

  • Suspicious sender address: The email came from a non-official domain instead of a verified CIBC address.
  • Urgency and fear tactics: The message pressured the user to act quickly to avoid account suspension.
  • Unexpected login warning: The user was not expecting password attempts, which is a common phishing trigger.
  • External link risk: The reset button directed to a non-official page rather than a verified banking site.

What Should Have Been Done Instead

  • Do not click links in unexpected security emails.
  • Manually go to the official CIBC website instead of using email links.
  • Verify the sender by checking the full email address carefully.
  • Contact the bank or IT support directly using trusted phone numbers or official websites.
  • Report the email as phishing using your email provider tools.
