Ransomware Explained

What you have just experienced is an example of ransomware.

After downloading malicious software onto a computer, attackers can encrypt files and lock users out of their own data. Encryption means the files become unreadable unless they are unlocked with a special decryption key controlled by the attacker.

Many users and companies store important documents, photos, passwords, and financial records on their devices, which is why some victims choose to pay the ransom.

Malware is a major cybersecurity threat for businesses and organizations around the world, and ransomware attacks continue to increase every year.

Always double check the sender of emails and verify websites before downloading software or clicking suspicious links.
