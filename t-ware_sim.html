Trojan Horse Malware Explained

What you have just experienced is an example of a Trojan Horse.

Trojan malware conceals itself as legitimate software in order to trick users into voluntarily downloading and installing it onto their device, often using social endineering.

Unlike worms or viruses, Trojan malware often relies heavily on social engineering, including urgency, fear, and authority, to convince victims that the software is safe or required. Malware could be any form of malware.

Once installed, Trojan malware may secretly disable security protections, steal banking information, monitor activity, provide remote access to attackers, or download additional malware onto the system.

In this simulation, the fake “banking security plugin” silently gained access behind the scenes while appearing normal to the user.

Always verify emails, avoid downloading attachments from suspicious senders, and contact organizations directly before installing unknown software.
