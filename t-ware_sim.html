Trojan Horse Malware Explained

What you have just experienced is an example of a Trojan Horse attack.

Trojan malware disguises itself as legitimate software in order to trick users into downloading and installing it voluntarily.

Once installed, a Trojan may secretly gain system access, steal passwords, monitor activity, access banking information, create remote backdoors, or download additional malware onto the computer.

Unlike worms or viruses, Trojans rely heavily on social engineering techniques such as urgency, fear, and pretending to be trusted companies or services.

In this simulation, the fake email attempted to create urgency by threatening banking access suspension within 24 hours.

Always verify email senders, avoid downloading attachments from suspicious emails, and contact organizations directly before installing unknown software.
