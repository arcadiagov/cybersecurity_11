Virus Malware Explained

What you have just experienced is an example of what a computer virus may attempt to do to a device.

Viruses are often designed to spread themselves into files, programs, or systems while causing as much disruption and destruction as possible.

A virus may rename or delete files, corrupt programs, spread itself to email contacts, steal information, or damage important operating system components.

Some viruses are designed purely for destruction, while others may focus on stealing personal or financial information.

If you suspect your computer has been infected by a virus, disconnect from Wi-Fi or the network immediately to help prevent the malware from spreading to other devices.

Then contact a reputable IT department or cybersecurity professional for assistance removing the malware safely.
